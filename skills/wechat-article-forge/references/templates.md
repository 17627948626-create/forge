# Article Templates

Treat this file as a **route library**, not a rigid skeleton library.

The active rule is:

- pick the route that best matches the angle and the author's natural movement
- feel free to deviate from the route if the article becomes more authored, clearer, or more concrete
- do not force every article through the same "background →观点→证据→总结" chassis

> 草稿 frontmatter ≠ 发布 frontmatter
> Draft/frontmatter examples here are only for writing-stage metadata. Final `publish.md` must still follow the publish contract in `SKILL.md`.

## How To Choose A Route

Prefer the route that best answers:

1. What is the article really trying to do: explain, persuade, reframe, or react?
2. Where is the strongest author-presence move: opening judgment, mid-article turn, or concrete ending choice?
3. Does the piece need a hot-news entry, a scene entry, or a consequence entry?

If the profile naturally writes scene-first, judgment-first, or with a delayed reveal, let the route follow that. Do not "correct" it back into a platform-generic structure.

## 资讯（News / Updates）

Target length: 800–1500 Chinese characters.

### Route A: Hot Hook → What Changed → Why It Matters

Use when the event itself is already strong enough to open the article.

1. Open with the thing that happened and why it matters now.
2. Briefly explain what changed relative to the prior state.
3. Pull out the one or two consequences that matter to this audience.
4. End on a concrete watchpoint, cost, or unresolved choice.

### Route B: Reader Cost First → Event As Evidence

Use when the event is only a trigger and the real value is the reader-facing implication.

1. Open with the concrete cost, pressure, or confusion readers now face.
2. Bring in the current event as proof that this shift is real.
3. Sort signal from noise with 2–4 evidence points.
4. Close on what the reader should now watch, stop, or do.

### Route C: Misread Narrative → Corrective Reading

Use when the mainstream take is too shallow.

1. Open with the common reading of the news.
2. Show why that reading is incomplete or wrong.
3. Rebuild the event through business, market, or user constraints.
4. End on the more accurate frame.

### 资讯 writing notes

- One hot hook is enough; do not turn it into a roundup unless the article type is actually `周报`.
- `style_exemplar_pack` can shape the opening and turns, but facts still come from `research.json`.
- Avoid interchangeable endings like “这件事值得持续关注”.

## 周报（Weekly Roundup）

Target length: 1000–2000 Chinese characters.

Recommended routes:

- `Editorial lead → top items → tools/resources → next watch`
- `One-week thesis → items grouped by theme → one-line judgments → next watch`

Weekly pieces may stay structurally stable, but the editorial lead should still sound like a specific author, not a newsletter template.

## 教程（Tutorial / How-To）

Target length: 1500–3000 Chinese characters.

Recommended routes:

- `pain point → outcome preview → prerequisites → steps → pitfalls → next step`
- `wrong way people try first → correct workflow → walkthrough → troubleshooting`

Use structure generously here, but let the "pitfalls" and "what actually matters" sections carry author presence.

## 观点（Opinion / Analysis）

Target length: 1200–2500 Chinese characters.

### Route A: Judgment First → Stakes → Evidence → Harder Conclusion

Use when the author has a sharp stance and evidence is ready.

1. State the judgment early.
2. Explain why this matters now.
3. Bring in the strongest evidence or cases.
4. Return with a sharper, more specific conclusion than the opening.

### Route B: Mainstream View → Turn → Rebuild The Frame

Use when the article's value lies in correcting a dominant but weak framing.

1. Fairly state the mainstream position.
2. Identify the missing assumption or blind spot.
3. Rebuild the issue from a more concrete frame.
4. Close on the choice, cost, or implication readers now need to confront.

### Route C: Scene / Symptom → Broader Judgment

Use when a small event or detail reveals a larger pattern.

1. Open with a scene, quote, user complaint, or concrete symptom.
2. Show why that detail is not isolated.
3. Expand into the larger structural judgment.
4. End on the practical consequence, not a slogan.

### 观点 writing notes

- Opinion pieces should be allowed to be looser than a textbook essay.
- Do not force a mandatory “主流观点—但是—结论—留问” path every time.
- If the author naturally starts with a scene or a jab, preserve that.
- Endings should land on a decision, tradeoff, or implication, not banner language.

## 科普（Explainer / Education）

Target length: 1500–3000 Chinese characters.

Recommended routes:

- `confusing phenomenon → mental model → mechanism → implication`
- `common misconception → correction → example → takeaway`

The core win is clarity. The authored layer should show up through examples, judgment, and framing, not through fake performance.

## Route Selection Cheat Sheet

Choose based on the real motion of the piece:

- pick `资讯 Route A` when the event is the headline
- pick `资讯 Route B` when the audience consequence is the headline
- pick `资讯 Route C` when the public discourse is misreading the event
- pick `观点 Route A` when the author's stance is already the engine
- pick `观点 Route B` when the article is mainly a reframing move
- pick `观点 Route C` when one detail can open a larger argument
